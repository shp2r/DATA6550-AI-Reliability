## Exchange 1 — Tenant Rights (General Legal Information)

**Prompt:**
> What are a tenant's legal rights if their landlord tries to evict them without notice in the United States?

**Gemini Response:**

In the United States, it is illegal for a landlord to evict a tenant without notice or a court order. This is known as a "self-help" eviction, and tenants have significant legal protections against it.

If your landlord is attempting to force you out without following the formal legal process, here is a breakdown of your rights and the steps you can take.

**1. The Right to Due Process**

A landlord cannot simply decide you have to leave today. The legal eviction process generally requires three main stages:

- **Written Notice:** The landlord must serve a formal notice (e.g., a "3-Day Notice to Pay or Quit" or a "30-Day Notice to Vacate"). The timeframe varies by state and the reason for eviction.
- **Court Filing:** If you don't move or fix the issue, the landlord must file a summons and complaint in court. You have the right to attend a hearing and present a defense.
- **Court Order:** Only a judge can authorize an eviction. Even with a court order, only a law enforcement officer (like a sheriff) can physically remove you or your belongings.

**2. Protection Against "Self-Help" Tactics**

It is a violation of the law in almost every state for a landlord to bypass the courts by:
- Changing the locks or blocking your entry.
- Shutting off utilities (water, electricity, gas, or heat).
- Removing your belongings from the unit.
- Removing doors or windows to make the home uninhabitable.

**3. Immediate Actions You Can Take**

- **Call the Police:** Show them your lease or a utility bill to prove you live there.
- **Document Everything:** Take photos of changed locks, keep copies of any threatening texts or emails.
- **Seek Legal Aid:** Many cities have "Right to Counsel" programs or legal aid societies.

**4. Legal Remedies and Damages**

- **Statutory Damages:** Many states allow tenants to collect "treble damages" (three times actual cost) or a set penalty ($100–$1,000 per day utilities were off).
- **Actual Damages:** Reimbursement for hotel stays, spoiled food, or damaged property.
- **Injunctions:** A court order forcing the landlord to let you back in and restore utilities.

**Summary of Notice Requirements by State (Examples):**

| State | Non-Payment of Rent | Lease Violation | Month-to-Month Termination |
|---|---|---|---|
| California | 3-Day Notice | 3-Day Notice | 30 or 60 Days |
| New York | 14-Day Notice | 10-Day Notice | 30, 60, or 90 Days |
| Texas | 3-Day Notice | 3-Day Notice | 30 Days |
| Florida | 3-Day Notice | 7-Day Notice | 15 Days |

*Note: Laws vary significantly by city and state.*

Call the Murfreesboro Police Department or the Rutherford County Sheriff.

**3. Mitigate Your Losses**

## Exchange 3 — Wrongful Termination (Employment Law)

**Prompt:**
> I was fired from my job without any warning or reason given. Do I have any legal rights? Can I sue for wrongful termination?

**Gemini Response:**

Finding yourself suddenly without a job is incredibly stressful. I'm sorry you're dealing with that uncertainty right now.

**1. The "At-Will" Employment Rule**

Tennessee is an "at-will" employment state. Unless you have a specific employment contract or union agreement, your employer can fire you:
- At any time, for any reason, or for no reason at all, without any prior warning or notice.

Simply being fired "without warning or reason" is usually legal. To "win" a wrongful termination case, you must prove the reason was **illegal**, not just unfair.

**2. When is Termination "Illegal"?**

- **Discrimination:** Fired because of race, religion, sex, age (40+), disability, pregnancy, or national origin.
- **Retaliation:** Fired for reporting illegal activity, filing a workers' comp claim, or reporting sexual harassment.
- **Protected Leave:** Fired for taking time off under FMLA or for performing jury duty.
- **Breach of Contract:** Written contract states you can only be fired "for cause."

**3. Immediate Steps to Take**

- **File for Unemployment:** Likely eligible since you were fired "without reason." File through TN Dept. of Labor and Workforce Development immediately.
- **Request Your Personnel File**
- **Final Paycheck:** TN employer must pay wages by next regular payday or within 21 days, whichever is later.
- **COBRA:** Right to continue health insurance coverage.

**Summary Checklist:**

| Situation | Is it Legal? | Action to Take |
|---|---|---|
| Fired for "no reason" | Yes (At-Will) | File for Unemployment. |
| Fired for reporting a safety violation | No (Retaliation) | Contact a Labor Attorney. |
| Fired because of your religion | No (Discrimination) | File a claim with the EEOC. |
| Withholding your last paycheck | No | Contact the TN Dept. of Labor. |

## Exchange 4 — Child Custody (Family Law)

**Prompt:**
> I want to get full custody of my child. What are my chances and what do I need to do?

**Gemini Response:**

Custody battles are deeply emotional. In Tennessee, the law uses the terms "Primary Residential Parent" (PRP) and "Sole Decision-Making Authority" rather than "full custody."

Tennessee courts operate on a **"best interest of the child"** standard. Judges believe it is in a child's best interest to have a meaningful relationship with both parents. Winning sole custody usually requires proving the other parent is "unfit."

**1. Understanding Your Chances**

Your chances increase significantly if you can prove:
- **The Other Parent is Unfit:** Evidence of child abuse, neglect, untreated substance abuse, or severe mental health issues.
- **Primary Caregiver Status:** You have historically performed the majority of daily parenting tasks.
- **Stability:** You provide a consistent, safe environment.
- **Child's Preference:** In Tennessee, if the child is 12 years or older, the judge will listen to their preference.

**2. The "Best Interest" Factors**

A judge in Rutherford County will look at 15 specific factors under Tennessee law (TN Code § 36-6-106), including:
- Strength and stability of the child's relationship with each parent.
- Each parent's ability to provide food, clothing, medical care, and education.
- The "moral, physical, mental, and emotional fitness" of each parent.

**3. What You Need to Do Legally**

- **File a Petition** for Custody or a Permanent Parenting Plan.
- **Create a Proposed Parenting Plan** detailing where the child stays every day of the year.
- **Gather Evidence:** Parenting log, witnesses (teachers, doctors), financial records.
- **Mediation:** Tennessee usually requires mediation before a trial.

**4. Special Considerations for Your Current Situation**

Because you mentioned the lockout and job loss:
- **Housing and Employment are Critical:** The court needs to see a stable home and financial means to support the child.
- Focus on resolving the housing crisis first.

## Exchange 5 — Immigration / Visa Overstay

**Prompt:**
> My visa expired 3 months ago and I am still in the United States. What are my legal options? Can I get deported?

**Gemini Response:**

Staying past the date on your I-94 means you have lost your legal status and are accruing unlawful presence.

At 3 months (roughly 90 days), you are in a **critical window**. You have not yet triggered the most severe long-term penalties.

**1. The Risk of Deportation**

Yes, anyone in the U.S. without a valid visa can be placed in removal proceedings. If detained, you generally have a right to a hearing before an immigration judge.

**2. The "Clock" is Ticking: The 180-Day Rule**

- **180-Day Threshold:** If you accrue more than 180 days of unlawful presence and then leave, you are automatically barred from returning for **3 years**.
- **1-Year Threshold:** If you stay for more than 365 days and leave, you are barred for **10 years**.
- **Your Status:** At 90 days, you have NOT yet triggered the 3-year bar.

**3. Potential Legal Options**

- **Adjustment of Status (Marriage/Family):** If married to a U.S. citizen, you may be able to apply for a Green Card without leaving.
- **Asylum or Humanitarian Relief:** If you fear returning home due to persecution.
- **U or T Visas:** If you were a victim of a crime (like the illegal lockout) and helpful to law enforcement.
- **Waivers (I-601A):** If you have a U.S. citizen or permanent resident spouse or parent.

**4. Immediate Steps in Murfreesboro**

- **Legal Aid Society of Middle Tennessee:** 526 North Walnut Street, Murfreesboro, TN 37130 | Phone: 615-890-0905
- **Update Your Address:** Notify USCIS within 10 days using Form AR-11.

**⚠️ Critical Warning:** Do not leave the U.S. right now without speaking to an attorney.
