# AI System Selection: Perplexity AI

## System Chosen
Perplexity AI  

## AI System Type
AI-powered search assistant which combines large language models with access to the web for real-time results & citation generation.

## Reason for Selection
I have selected Perplexity AI for this project because I have been an occasional user of the system since it launched. I am familiar with Perplexity AI through both my academic work (using it mainly to complete assignments) and my personal use for general information and everyday questions. Each of these types of usage has helped me to develop some understanding, based on practical experience, about how Perplexity AI performs when used in real-world settings; therefore, Perplexity AI has earned my vote as a strong contender for evaluation of reliability, consistency, and ethical considerations when generating responses from an AI.

## System Capabilities
- Answers provided through current online search  
- Answers provided with references  
- Summarizes information from news and other websites  
- Can respond to many different types of questions (academic, technical, general knowledge)  

## Known Limitations
- The system may misinterpret or incorrectly summarize an original source  
- The system may provide inaccurate or misleading reference information  
- Responses may differ according to available information  
- The system may have more difficulty answering highly specialized or ambiguous questions  
- The system may give an appearance of confidence when the answer is unsure  

## Testing Approach
The testing process will consist of structured tests that attempt to identify failure areas within the system. These tests will include:

1. Factual Accuracy Testing: Test to identify inaccuracies and false references  
2. Consistency Testing: Test to determine how consistent the response is to questions when the question is asked in the same way and in a different way  
3. Boundary Testing: Exploration of the limits of the system with regards to cutoff date, providing advice regarding legal or medical issues, and complex reasoning  
4. Edge Case Testing: Using ambiguous, conflicting and policy sensitive questions to observe system behavior  

The overall intent of this process will be to intentionally expose weaknesses identified in the testing of the system such as hallucinations, inconsistencies and ethical boundary violations that can be associated with the deployment of AI systems in real-life situations.
